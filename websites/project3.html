NOVA - Animal Shelter

case study

NOVA - Animal Shelter is a website and application project for a shelter for homeless dogs and cats in my hometown.

Project duration: March - April 2022

My role: UX researcher, UX designer

Responsibilities: user research, wireframing, prototyping, testing

Project overview

The problem:

  • I noticed that our shelter has huge communication problems, some of the community would like to help but are unable to because they do not even know that such help is needed.

The goal:

  • My goal is to improve communication between the shelter and the community willing to help.

User research: summary

  • I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users Iâ€™m designing for and their needs.
  • Overall, I distinguished two groups of people. The first group are people willing to help directly at the shelter, as volunteers. So far, these people have had problems with contact, they could not communicate with the shelter about the current needs, vacancies and schedules.
  • The second group of users are people who would simply like to help the shelter in other ways, adoption, but also material or financial help would be at stake. These people also have not been able to find the necessary information about what is needed in our shelter so far.
